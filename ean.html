Everything and Nothing at the Same Time is a short, surrealist found-footage film, created by Sasha Myshkina in the spring of 2021. Inspired by the techniques and methods that she had used in the creation of Untitled, this second, longer, film conveys a lot of similar ideas. However, despite the fact that Untitled had been inspired by the idea of a broken-up train of thought and the nature of duality, Everything and Nothing at the Same Time feels like a very separate entity - evoking feelings of stress, panic and speed. Organic sound effects of breathing and heartbeats lend an almost ASMR-like quality to the film.

Additionally, whilst Untitled, despite containing several deliberately-filmed clips, was still very solidly a found-footage film in the most traditional sense, almost all of this film was shot by Myshkina, on her phone, for the specific occassion, with only a few found clips. However, despite this, it does play a lot like Untitled with its cross-cutting of seemingly unrelated things, blurring the definition of what a found-footage film truly is.

The almost-flippant warning at the start of the film - "The film you are about to see has no meaning" - conveys the main message of this film: that what it truly means is up to the viewer.
