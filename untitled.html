Untitled was the first short film that Sasha Myshkina made. It was created in the fall of 2020 for her art course, under the abstract brief of creating a film out of a various selection of found-footage from the Internet. However, in addition to this, Myshkina also used footage shot on her phone, all edited together to Rachmaninov's Concerto in C Minor.

Untitled was inspired by an interrupted train of thought - the way that abstract ideas, often completely unrelated, overlap and link into each otehr, often interrupting each other, as demonstarted by the jarring cut in the middle of the film. It also hints an almost Yin and Yang-esqueduality of colour, juxtaposed with clips matching the colours mentioned, which breaks at the end of the film - colour.

This film is not meant for interpretaton, with the viewer's peception of it meant to be as abstract as the footage it conveys.
